# Getting cheapheadsets.com.au Indexed and Ranking on Google

Your site already has solid basics: `robots.txt`, `sitemap.xml`, unique titles and meta descriptions, canonicals, `lang="en-AU"`, and Organisation + FAQ schema. Below is a focused plan for **indexing** and **ranking** in Australia.

---

## 1. Get indexed quickly

### Google Search Console (do this first)
1. Go to [Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console).
2. Add a property for **https://www.cheapheadsets.com.au** (or the exact URL you use).
3. Verify ownership (HTML file upload or DNS record as suggested).
4. **Submit your sitemap:** Sitemaps → Add sitemap → enter: `https://www.cheapheadsets.com.au/sitemap.xml` → Submit.
5. Use **URL Inspection** for your homepage and a few key pages (e.g. `/products.html`, one blog post). Click “Request indexing” so Google crawls them sooner.

### Consistency: www vs non-www
- Pick one canonical host (e.g. **www.cheapheadsets.com.au**) and use it everywhere: canonicals, sitemap, and links.
- Your sitemap and canonicals already use `https://www.cheapheadsets.com.au` — keep that consistent across the site and in Search Console.

---

## 2. Technical (already in good shape)

- **robots.txt** — Allows all; points to sitemap.
- **sitemap.xml** — Lists main, product, location, and blog URLs.
- **Canonical tags** — Each page should have one (you have these).
- **Titles and meta descriptions** — Unique per page, with keywords (you have these).
- **Mobile-friendly** — Responsive layout and viewport meta.
- **HTTPS** — Use it everywhere (Cloudflare provides this).

Optional improvement: add an **og:image** (e.g. your homepage hero or a logo card) on the homepage and key landing pages so shares look good; this can help click-through from social and some search features.

---

## 3. Content and on-page (ranking)

### Keywords you’re already targeting
- Cheap headsets, budget headsets, Bluetooth/USB/telephone headsets.
- Brand alternatives: Jabra, Plantronics/Poly, EPOS, Logitech, etc.
- Location: Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Adelaide, Canberra, Hobart + suburbs.
- Use these naturally in titles, headings, and first paragraph where they fit.

### Best practices
- **One main topic per page** — e.g. “USB headsets” on the USB page; “cheap headsets Sydney” on the Sydney page.
- **Clear H1 and H2s** — You already use these; keep them descriptive and keyword-relevant.
- **Internal links** — Link from homepage and blog to product and location pages (you do this); add a few more from blog posts to product category pages where relevant.
- **Australian spelling and audience** — You’re already using AU spelling and cities; keep that consistent.
- **Blog** — Keep adding articles (e.g. “alternatives to [model]”, “best budget headset for…”) and link them to product and contact pages.

### Content updates
- Add or refresh a few sentences on key pages every few months (e.g. product category intros, location pages).
- When you add new blog posts, add their URLs to `sitemap.xml` and optionally request indexing in Search Console for the most important ones.

---

## 4. Local and Australian relevance

- **.com.au** — Good for Australian intent; keep it as your main domain.
- **Location pages** — Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Adelaide, Canberra, Hobart (and suburbs in copy) help for “cheap headsets [city]” type queries.
- **Google Business Profile** — If you have a business address or service area, create/claim a [Google Business Profile](https://business.google.com) and link to cheapheadsets.com.au. Use the same NAP (name, address, phone) as on the site.
- **Local citations** — Same business name, address, and phone on other directories and partner sites (e.g. Telco Broker if they list you) help consistency and trust.

---

## 5. Off-page (links and trust)

- **Quality backlinks** — Links from relevant Australian business, tech, or industry sites help more than random links. Partner mentions (e.g. Telco Broker), directories, or a short press release can help.
- **Avoid** — Buying links, link farms, or stuffing keywords; focus on useful content and natural mentions.
- **Social** — Sharing key pages (homepage, product range, blog posts) can bring traffic and occasional links; not required for indexing but useful for visibility.

---

## 6. Speed and experience

- **Hosting** — Cloudflare Pages is fast; keep it.
- **Images** — You’re using local assets and optimization guidance; keep hero and product images compressed (e.g. 1600px wide, JPEG/WebP, quality 80–85).
- **Core Web Vitals** — Check in Search Console (Experience → Core Web Vitals) after the site is indexed; fix any “Poor” URLs (usually images or render-blocking resources).

---

## 7. What to do in the first week

1. Add the site in **Google Search Console** and verify.
2. **Submit** `https://www.cheapheadsets.com.au/sitemap.xml`.
3. **Request indexing** for the homepage and 3–5 important URLs (e.g. `/products.html`, one product page, one location, one blog post).
4. Confirm **canonical host** (www vs non-www) is the same in sitemap, canonicals, and Search Console.
5. Optionally add **og:image** on the homepage for better sharing.

---

## 8. Ongoing (ranking over time)

- **New content** — Add blog posts or update product/location pages periodically.
- **Internal links** — From new posts to existing product and location pages.
- **Sitemap** — Add new URLs when you add pages; re-submit sitemap in Search Console if you prefer (Google also picks up changes by crawling).
- **Search Console** — Check Performance (queries, pages, countries) and Coverage; fix any errors or “Indexed – page has issues” where needed.
- **Patience** — Indexing can take days to a few weeks; ranking improvements often take months. Consistency in content, technical setup, and a few good links will help most.

---

**Summary:** Use Google Search Console to submit the sitemap and request indexing for key URLs. Keep your technical setup (canonicals, sitemap, meta tags) consistent, focus on clear content and internal links, and support the site with a Business Profile and a few quality links. That’s the best approach to get cheapheadsets.com.au indexed and working toward better rankings in Australia.
