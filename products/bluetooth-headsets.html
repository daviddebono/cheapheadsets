Bluetooth Headsets

Bluetooth headsets give you cord-free freedom for calls and audio, whether you are at a desk, moving around the office or working from home. This page outlines what to look for in a budget Bluetooth headset and how value options such as Mairdi compare to mainstream brands.

Why choose a Bluetooth headset?

Bluetooth headsets connect to your computer, smartphone or tablet without cables. They are ideal for hybrid workers, mobile professionals and anyone who wants to move while on a call. Typical features include noise-cancelling microphones, multipoint pairing (connecting to two devices at once) and long battery life. Premium brands such as Jabra and Plantronics (Poly) are known for advanced noise reduction and strong connectivity; EPOS and Logitech also offer well-regarded business models. If you do not need the very latest features or brand prestige, budget Bluetooth headsets can deliver solid performance at a lower price.

Mainstream brands vs budget options

Jabra is recognised for innovative wireless technology and strong adoption in offices and call centres. Plantronics (now Poly by HP) has been a pioneer in professional headsets since the 1960s, with popular lines such as Savi and Voyager. EPOS brings Sennheiser-derived audio quality and technologies such as BrainAdapt™. Logitech has grown quickly with comfortable business headsets and good integration with video collaboration tools. These brands command a premium. For many users, a value-focused Bluetooth headset from a supplier such as Mairdi can meet everyday needs: clear voice, reliable pairing and comfortable wear. Mairdi headsets are available through our partner; we provide information and direct links so you can view and purchase the right model for your needs.

What to look for in a budget Bluetooth headset

When comparing options, consider battery life (typical ranges are several hours to a full day of use), comfort for long wearing sessions, microphone quality and noise cancellation level, and compatibility with your devices (PC, Mac, smartphone). Check whether the headset supports multipoint if you need to switch between two devices. For business use, a boom microphone or well-placed mic is important for clear voice pickup. Build quality and warranty are also worth checking—budget does not have to mean flimsy.
